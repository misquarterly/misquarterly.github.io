Welcome

I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Business at Wake Forest University.

I received my PhD from the MIS Department at Terry College of Business, University of Georgia. My dissertation focuses on understanding the use of bots in social movements. Bots are computer algorithms sharing messages, uploading pictures, and connecting with many users on places like Twitter and Facebook and they can be used in a variety of ways, as for example, to explore the dark web and to disseminate live news about earthquakes. I obtained my master's (in Economics) and bachelor's (in Language & International Trade) degrees at Clemson University.

My current research aims at generating insights to improve social interactions with computer algorithms in ethical ways. Specifically, I am interested in (1) illustrating how bots disseminate information in online social networks; (2) generating theory on the use of conversational agents in an increasingly hyper-private web browsing environment; and (3) understanding and disentangling the complex nature of algorithmic transparency.
